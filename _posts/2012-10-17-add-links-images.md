Note: These instructions were written for WordPress version 3.3 on Trinity Commons. For newer versions on other systems, see the general instructions on <a title="codex" href="http://codex.wordpress.org/" target="_blank">WordPress Codex</a>.

**To add a link to text** in your WordPress editor, select the text and click the link button.
  
[<img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-781" alt="" src="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/LinkButton.png" width="483" height="127" srcset="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/LinkButton.png 483w, http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/LinkButton-300x79.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 483px) 100vw, 483px" />](http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/LinkButton.png)On the next screen, insert the destination of the link and add a simple title. If the link points to an external site, and you would like readers to easily return to your site, click the box to &#8220;Open link in a new window/tab.&#8221;

[<img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-782" alt="" src="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/LinkScreen.png" width="479" height="231" srcset="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/LinkScreen.png 479w, http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/LinkScreen-300x145.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 479px) 100vw, 479px" />](http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/LinkScreen.png)

**To add an image** to your WordPress content, identify an image that already exists on the web (with a URL), or <a title="screenshot" href="http://commons.trincoll.edu/jackdougherty/2012/09/27/screenshot/" target="_blank">capture a screenshot</a> or obtain an image to upload to your site. Be sure to follow relevant copyright guidelines and credit images to their original source.

In the WordPress editor, place the cursor in front of the relevant text, and click the upload/insert media button.

[<img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-785" alt="" src="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/UploadMedia.png" width="177" height="128" />](http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/UploadMedia.png)

On the Add Media screen, drag & drop your image file into the box, OR click the &#8220;From URL&#8221; tab if the image already exists elsewhere on the web, OR click &#8220;Media Library&#8221; if you have previously uploaded the image to your site.

[<img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-786" alt="" src="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/AddMediaScreen.png" width="590" height="313" srcset="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/AddMediaScreen.png 590w, http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/AddMediaScreen-300x159.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 590px) 100vw, 590px" />](http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/AddMediaScreen.png)

At the bottom of the Add Media screen, add a caption (especially to credit the original source), choose alignment and size, and click &#8220;Insert into post.&#8221;

[<img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-790" alt="" src="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/AddMediaScreenCaption.png" width="545" height="650" srcset="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/AddMediaScreenCaption.png 545w, http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/AddMediaScreenCaption-252x300.png 252w" sizes="(max-width: 545px) 100vw, 545px" />](http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/AddMediaScreenCaption.png)

**To edit an image, wrap text, or change its destination URL**, select the image, float the cursor to the top-left corner, and select the edit button (a small landscape photo).

[<img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-793" alt="" src="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/EditImage.png" width="570" height="197" srcset="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/EditImage.png 570w, http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/EditImage-300x104.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 570px) 100vw, 570px" />](http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/EditImage.png)

On the Edit Image tab, resize the image and modify alignment. To wrap text, be sure to have placed the cursor immediately before the paragraph when inserting the image.

If you wish to send users who click on the image to a new destination on the web, insert the web address in to the Link URL field. On the &#8220;Advanced Settings&#8221; tab, click &#8220;Open link in a new window.&#8221;

[<img class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-794" alt="" src="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/WrapText.png" width="599" height="518" srcset="http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/WrapText.png 599w, http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/WrapText-300x259.png 300w" sizes="(max-width: 599px) 100vw, 599px" />](http://localhost/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/10/WrapText.png)
